Girl on a Swing - JPS

Not so much is known about this piece but although it is believed by many to be a geniune Banksy, it is infact a piece by the almost-equally-well-known JPS (Jamie Paul Scanlon). This stencil based piece was sprayed in 2013 and it clearly draws on Banksy’s style and subject matter, but that is no surprise as JPS credits his emergence and confidence as a street artist with a visit he made to a Banksy exhibition at Bristol Museum.

Paint Pot Angel - Banksy

This piece was created back in 2009 when Banksy had collaborated with the Bristol Museum and Art Gallery on an exhibition called Banksy versus Bristol Museum. In true Banksy style, it featured a burnt-out ice cream van on the Museum’s front steps and fishfingers floating in a goldfish bowl. Most famous of all was the sculpture of an angel, which stands in the Museum’s sculpture hall, over whose head Banksy had dumped a can of pink paint, leaving the can in place. This became known as ‘Paint Pot Angel’. In fact, the piece became so iconic that the Museum has left it untouched over the past eight years.

Spartacus - JPS

This piece created by the artist JPS was made to pay tribute to the late Welsh actor Andy Whitfield who died aged 39 in 2011 after an 18-month battle with non-Hodgkin’s lymphoma. Whitfield can be seen in his most well-known role as Spartacus in the Starz television series on the side of the Queen Shilling on Frogmore Street.

Well Hung Lover - Banksy

This is probably the most well known pice of street art in Bristol. Also known as Naked Man Hanging From Window, or just Naked Man, Banksy’s Well Hung Lover adds a bit of unexpected art to a sexual health clinic. The famous, anonymous street artist painted it in 2006, and it later became the United Kingdom’s first piece of legal street art. As with most graffiti, the city council originally wasn’t too keen on the unsanctioned artwork, but locals supported it. When the council sent out an online poll about whether to remove it or not, 97% of the respondents voted in favor of letting Banksy’s art stay. Unfortunately, even the council’s eventual approval couldn’t completely protect the renowned street artist’s work. In June 2009, vandals fired blue paint balls at the image and defaced it. Most of the resulting blurry blobs of paint were removed, though the bits of paint that actually hit the image were left since removing them would further damage the mural. In 2018, it was vandalized yet again when someone sprayed rude graffiti tags on the wall.

Queen Jubilee - Banksy

This piece is strongly believed to be a Banksy piece but this has not been completely confirmed. The piece shows the crown-wearing monarch sporting a jagged red stripe, just like the 1970s David Bowie creation. The piece sprang up as the country celebrated her 60 years on the throne and appeared on a wall that has already been used by Banksy.
